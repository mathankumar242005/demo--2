# Apple Website – Product Requirements Document (PRD)

## 1. Product Overview

### Product Name

Apple.com (Global Marketing & Commerce Website)

### Purpose

The Apple website is the **primary digital storefront and brand experience** for Apple’s products and services. It combines **marketing, education, commerce, and support** into a single, cohesive platform.

### Target Users

* **Consumers** (new & existing Apple customers)
* **Professionals & enterprises**
* **Students & educators**
* **Developers**
* **Support seekers**

---

## 2. Goals & Objectives

### Business Goals

* Drive **product discovery and sales**
* Reinforce **premium brand perception**
* Reduce dependency on third‑party retailers
* Increase attachment of **services** (iCloud, Apple Music, AppleCare, etc.)

### User Goals

* Easily understand products & differences
* Confidently compare and purchase
* Access support without friction
* Feel trust, quality, and simplicity

---

## 3. Core Value Proposition

> “A simple, elegant, and trustworthy way to explore, buy, and get support for Apple products.”

Key differentiators:

* Minimalist design
* Clear storytelling
* Seamless buying experience
* Tight ecosystem integration

---

## 4. Key User Journeys

### 4.1 Product Discovery

1. User lands on homepage
2. Scrolls through featured launches
3. Clicks a product (e.g., iPhone)
4. Learns features via visuals & copy

### 4.2 Product Comparison

1. User opens comparison table
2. Compares models, specs, pricing
3. Chooses best‑fit product

### 4.3 Purchase Flow

1. Selects product configuration
2. Adds accessories & AppleCare
3. Logs into Apple ID
4. Completes checkout

### 4.4 Support Journey

1. Visits Support section
2. Selects device/service
3. Accesses guides or contacts support

---

## 6. Non‑Functional Requirements

### Performance

* Fast page load (<2s)
* Optimized images & animations

### Accessibility

* WCAG compliance
* Keyboard navigation
* Screen reader support

### Security

* Secure checkout (PCI compliant)
* Apple ID authentication
* Data encryption

### Scalability

* Global traffic handling
* Localized content & pricing

---

## 7. Design Principles

* Simplicity over complexity
* Content‑first layouts
* High contrast & whitespace
* Emotion‑driven storytelling
* Consistency across devices

---

## 8. Metrics & Success Criteria

### Business Metrics

* Conversion rate

### User Metrics

* Support resolution rate

## 9. Assumptions & Constraints

### Assumptions

* Users trust Apple as a premium brand
* Most users browse before visiting a store

### Constraints

* Must align with Apple brand guidelines
* Global legal & tax compliance

## 10. Future Enhancements

## 11. Summary

The Apple website is not just an e‑commerce platform—it is a **digital expression of Apple’s brand**. Every interaction is designed to reduce friction, build trust, and guide users from curiosity to confident purchase.
